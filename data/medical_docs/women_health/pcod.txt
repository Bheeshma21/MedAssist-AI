Disease: Polycystic Ovarian Disease (PCOD)

Overview:
Polycystic Ovarian Disease (PCOD) is a hormonal disorder in which the ovaries produce immature or partially mature eggs that develop into cysts. It commonly affects women of reproductive age.

Common Symptoms:
- Irregular menstrual cycles
- Weight gain
- Acne
- Excess facial or body hair
- Hair thinning
- Difficulty becoming pregnant
- Oily skin

Risk Factors:
- Family history
- Obesity
- Insulin resistance
- Sedentary lifestyle

Diagnosis:
- Medical history
- Physical examination
- Hormone blood tests
- Pelvic ultrasound

Lifestyle Management:
- Regular exercise
- Healthy balanced diet
- Weight reduction if overweight
- Stress management
- Adequate sleep

Medical Treatment:
- Oral contraceptive pills
- Metformin
- Ovulation induction medicines
- Hormonal therapy depending on symptoms

Diet Recommendations:
- High fiber foods
- Whole grains
- Vegetables
- Lean protein
- Reduce refined sugar
- Avoid sugary beverages

When to Consult a Doctor:
- Missed periods for several months
- Severe pelvic pain
- Excessive bleeding
- Fertility concerns
- Sudden severe abdominal pain

Emergency Symptoms:
Seek immediate medical attention if severe abdominal pain, fainting, or heavy uncontrolled bleeding occurs.
