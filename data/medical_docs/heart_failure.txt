Heart Failure

Heart failure is a chronic condition in which the heart cannot pump blood effectively.

Common symptoms:
- Shortness of breath
- Orthopnea (difficulty breathing while lying flat)
- Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea
- Fatigue
- Reduced exercise tolerance
- Swelling of legs and ankles
- Rapid weight gain due to fluid retention

Risk factors:
- Previous myocardial infarction
- Hypertension
- Diabetes mellitus
- Coronary artery disease
- Smoking
- Obesity

Recommended investigations:
- ECG
- Echocardiogram
- BNP blood test
- Chest X-ray
- Troponin

Initial treatment:
- Diuretics
- ACE inhibitors
- Beta blockers
- Lifestyle modification
